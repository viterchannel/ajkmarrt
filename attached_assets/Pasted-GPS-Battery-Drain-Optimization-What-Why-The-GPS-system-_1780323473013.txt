GPS Battery Drain Optimization
What & Why
The GPS system currently pings every 10 seconds unconditionally, draining a rider's battery in 4–6 hours. Riders who are stationary (waiting for a request) or offline should not be tracked at the same frequency as riders actively on a trip.

Done looks like
When a rider has not moved more than 10 meters in the last 60 seconds, ping interval increases to 30 seconds automatically
When the rider goes offline (toggle set to offline), GPS pinging stops entirely and resumes when they go online again
During an active ride/order (in_transit status), interval stays at the fast 10-second rate for accuracy
Battery-friendly behaviour is transparent to the rider (no UI change needed)
GPS pings resume immediately at the fast rate when the device starts moving again (detected via devicemotion or position delta)
Out of scope
Native Capacitor Geofencing API (future enhancement for native Android build)
Changing the backend GPS ingestion rate or schema
User-facing settings for GPS interval
Steps
Add stationary detection to gpsQueue.ts — Track the last N positions and compute total distance moved. If distance < 10m over the last 60 seconds, switch the ping interval to 30s. Reset to 10s immediately when movement is detected again (position delta > 10m).

Pause GPS when rider is offline — Listen to the rider's online/offline status from the auth or socket context. When the rider sets themselves offline, clear the GPS ping interval and stop sending location updates. Re-start the interval when they go back online.

Lock to fast interval during active ride — When the socket receives ride:accepted, order:accepted, or status transitions to in_transit, force the interval to 10s regardless of stationary detection, and hold it there until ride:completed or order:delivered.

Add a visibility-based pause — When document.visibilityState === 'hidden' and the rider is not on an active trip, reduce pings to 60s (PWA background tab scenario) to avoid unnecessary battery drain from background tabs.
